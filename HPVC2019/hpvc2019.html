HPVC North 2019

I helped start the Harvard College Human Powered Vehicle Team (HPVT) back in fall of 2017. Our mission was to design and fabricate a new human-powered vehicle each year to compete in the American Society of Mechanical Engineers Human Powered Vehicle Challenge (HPVC). The project is a way for Harvard engineering undergraduates to learn hands-on engineering and teamwork skills that are not covered in classes.

In the 2017-2018 academic year, my sophomore year, we did not finish our recumbent tricycle, Crimson Cruiser, in time for competition. We presented a half-finished vehicle at the spring design fair and got a nice article written about us.

Crimson Cruiser, last year's incomplete vehicle

Failing to finish something that you promised yourself you would finish is a terrible feeling. Determined not to feel that way again, I went into my junior year completely certain that we would compete in 2019. I was elected vice president of the club over the summer of 2018 and promoted to president at the beginning of 2019.
