Physics 125: Widely Applied Physics

Final Project: The Physics of Bowling

Phyics 125: Widely Applied Physics (formerly Physics for Future Presidential Advisors) is an undergraduate-level phyiscs class taught by Professor David Morin at Harvard. He was quite possibly the best teacher I had. The class teaches you how to be 80% right with 20% of the work using dimensional analysis and good estimations. You learn about solid mechanics, the greenhouse effect, why highway off-ramps are the shape they are, general relativity, and how many gallons of peanut butter you'd need to eat to bike across the United States (answer: four or five).

If you're a big time Alex Giglio fan and remember my beer-pong playing robot and shock absorber, then you might remember Terrance "TJ" Williams, my partner on the final project for Physics 125. The assignment was to discuss a complicated topic in physics in simple terms. We made this video about bowling. Please enjoy.
